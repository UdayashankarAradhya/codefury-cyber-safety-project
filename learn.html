Knowledge is the best defense. Here are common scams to watch out for.

Common Scams Everyone Should Know

Phishing Scams

Scammers send fraudulent emails or messages impersonating reputable companies to trick you into revealing personal information, such as passwords, credit card numbers, and bank account details.

How to Avoid:
  • Never click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown senders.
  • Check the sender's email address for authenticity.
  • Look for grammatical errors and urgent, threatening language.

UPI/Digital Payment Scams

Scammers use various tactics, such as fake payment requests or QR codes, to trick you into authorizing payments from your account instead of receiving money.

How to Avoid:
  • Remember: You never need to enter your PIN to *receive* money.
  • Never scan a QR code unless you are making a payment to a trusted merchant.
  • Be wary of messages creating false urgency.

Fake OTP/SMS Scams

Fraudsters send messages claiming to be from your bank or a service provider, asking you to share a One-Time Password (OTP) to "verify" your account or "prevent" a block. Sharing the OTP gives them access to your account.

How to Avoid:
  • Never share your OTP with anyone, including bank employees or customer support.
  • Read the OTP message carefully; it usually states what the OTP is for.

Fake Lottery & Prize Scams

You receive an email or SMS claiming you've won a large sum of money or a prize. To claim it, you are asked to pay a "processing fee" or "tax." The prize does not exist.

How to Avoid:
  • If you didn't enter a contest, you didn't win.
  • Legitimate lotteries do not ask for a fee to release winnings.

Tech Support Scams

A pop-up window or phone call claims your computer is infected with a virus. The "technician" asks for remote access to your device and then charges you for unnecessary services or installs malware.

How to Avoid:
  • Legitimate tech companies will not contact you unsolicited.
  • Never grant remote access to your computer to someone you don't know and trust.

Scams Targeting Students

Easy Money/Part-time Job Scams

These scams promise high-paying, flexible part-time jobs that require little effort. Often, you are asked to pay an upfront "registration fee" or "training cost," or to process payments through your bank account, which could be part of a money-laundering scheme.

How to Avoid:
  • Be skeptical of jobs that offer high pay for simple tasks.
  • Never pay a fee to secure a job. Legitimate employers do not charge for hiring.
  • Do not use your personal bank account for company transactions.

Scams Targeting Teachers

Fake Government Schemes & Benefits

Scammers create fake websites or send messages about exclusive government schemes, grants, or training programs for educators. They aim to steal personal information or collect "application fees" for non-existent benefits.

How to Avoid:
  • Always verify schemes on official government websites (ending in .gov.in or .nic.in).
  • Do not trust unsolicited messages about special benefits.
  • Government bodies rarely ask for fees through unofficial payment links.

Scams Targeting Senior Citizens

Fake Medical Emergency Scams

A scammer calls or messages, pretending to be a grandchild or other relative in urgent need of money for a medical emergency. They insist on secrecy and immediate payment, creating panic to bypass rational thinking.

How to Avoid:
  • Resist the pressure to act immediately.
  • Hang up and call your relative back on their known, trusted phone number to verify the story.
  • Ask a personal question that only your real relative would know the answer to.

Scams Targeting Working Professionals

Investment & Trading Scams

These scams promise guaranteed, high returns on investments in stocks, cryptocurrency, or other financial products. They often use fake apps or websites that show fabricated profits, luring you to invest more before disappearing with your money.

How to Avoid:
  • If an investment promises guaranteed high returns with zero risk, it's a scam.
  • Only use reputable, well-known investment platforms and brokers.
  • Beware of "advisors" who contact you on social media or messaging apps.

Scams Targeting Parents

Fake School/College Admission Scams

Scammers pose as agents who can guarantee admission into a prestigious school or college for a large "donation" or "processing fee." They exploit the anxiety of parents to extract money for a service they cannot provide.

How to Avoid:
  • Follow the official admission process published by the educational institution.
  • Never pay cash for admissions or deal with unauthorized "agents."
  • Verify any payment requests directly with the institution's official admissions office.
