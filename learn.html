🚨 Common Scams Everyone Should Know

💳 UPI/Digital Payment Scams

Scammers trick you into sending money through UPI apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, or Paytm by pretending to be someone trustworthy or creating fake emergencies.

How to Stay Safe:

  • Never send money to unknown numbers, even if they claim to be friends or family in distress
  • Always verify through a phone call before sending money to anyone
  • Banks never ask for money transfers to "verify" your account
  • Double-check the recipient's name before confirming any payment

📱 Fake OTP/SMS Scams

Scammers send fake messages claiming to be from your bank, asking you to share OTPs (One-Time Passwords) or click malicious links to "update" your account.

How to Stay Safe:

  • Never share OTPs with anyone - even bank employees won't ask for them
  • Banks never send links via SMS asking you to update KYC or account details
  • Always contact your bank directly using official numbers if you receive suspicious messages
  • Look for spelling mistakes and unofficial sender numbers in messages

🎰 Fake Lottery & Prize Scams

You receive messages saying you've won a huge lottery or prize from companies like KBC, Flipkart, or international lotteries you never entered.

How to Stay Safe:

  • You cannot win a lottery you never participated in
  • Legitimate prizes never require you to pay fees or taxes upfront
  • Real companies contact winners through official channels, not random WhatsApp messages
  • If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is

🎓 Scams Targeting Students

💰 Easy Money/Part-time Job Scams

Fake job offers promising easy money for simple tasks like data entry, survey filling, or social media engagement. They ask for registration fees or personal documents.

Student-Specific Safety Tips:

  • Legitimate part-time jobs never ask for upfront payment
  • Be cautious of "work from home" offers that seem too easy
  • Never share your Aadhaar card, PAN card, or bank details for "verification"
  • Ask seniors or parents before taking any online job offers

🎮 Gaming & App Scams

Fake gaming apps or betting platforms promising guaranteed wins and easy money. Popular among students looking for quick cash.

Stay Safe While Gaming:

  • Never download APK files from unknown sources
  • Avoid betting or gambling apps - they're designed to make you lose money
  • Don't share gaming account passwords with strangers
  • Be cautious of in-app purchases and subscription traps

📚 Educational Loan & Scholarship Scams

Fake offers for instant education loans or scholarships that require processing fees or personal information.

Verify Before You Trust:

  • Research scholarship providers through official government websites
  • Legitimate scholarships never ask for processing fees
  • Always involve parents/guardians in financial decisions
  • Use only official bank channels for education loans

👩‍🏫 Scams Targeting Teachers

📋 Fake Government Schemes & Benefits

Scammers impersonate government officials offering fake teacher benefits, pension updates, or salary increments requiring personal information.

Verify Government Communications:

  • Always verify through official school administration or education department
  • Government schemes are announced through official channels, not WhatsApp
  • Never share employee ID, PAN, or bank details over phone/messages
  • Check government websites for authentic scheme information

💻 Educational Platform & Training Scams

Fake offers for expensive teaching certifications, online courses, or educational software with upfront payments and false promises.

Choose Legitimate Education Platforms:

  • Research the organization thoroughly before paying for any course
  • Check reviews and ratings from multiple sources
  • Prefer recognized institutions over unknown online platforms
  • Be wary of courses promising unrealistic career transformations

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Parent Impersonation Scams

Scammers pretend to be parents of students, requesting personal information about children or creating fake emergencies.

Protect Student Information:

  • Always verify parent identity through school records before sharing any information
  • Don't share student personal details over phone or messages
  • Follow school protocols for parent communication
  • Be cautious of urgent requests for money or information

👴 Scams Targeting Senior Citizens

🏥 Fake Medical Emergency Scams

Scammers call pretending your family member is in hospital and needs immediate money transfer for treatment.

Handle Emergency Calls Carefully:

  • Always call your family member directly before sending any money
  • Ask specific questions only your family would know
  • Take time to verify - real emergencies allow for a quick verification call
  • Involve other family members before making large money transfers

💊 Health & Medicine Scams

Fake miracle cures, expensive health supplements, or medical schemes targeting health-conscious seniors.

Protect Your Health & Money:

  • Consult your regular doctor before trying any new treatment
  • Be skeptical of "miracle cures" or "guaranteed" treatments
  • Buy medicines only from licensed pharmacies
  • Don't fall for high-pressure sales tactics

🏛️ Fake Bank Official Scams

Scammers pose as bank managers or RBI officials, claiming account problems that need immediate attention and money transfers.

Verify Bank Communications:

  • Banks never ask for money transfers to fix account issues
  • Visit your bank branch personally for any major account concerns
  • Don't download apps sent by unknown callers
  • Keep a trusted family member informed about financial matters

💼 Scams Targeting Working Professionals

💹 Investment & Trading Scams

Fake stock tips, cryptocurrency investments, or trading platforms promising guaranteed high returns on investments.

Invest Wisely:

  • Research any investment platform thoroughly before investing
  • No investment can guarantee returns without risk
  • Use only SEBI-registered investment platforms
  • Be wary of pressure to invest immediately

🏢 Fake Job Offers & LinkedIn Scams

Fraudulent job offers requiring upfront fees for "processing" or fake companies requesting personal documents and payments.

Verify Job Opportunities:

  • Research the company on official websites and LinkedIn
  • Legitimate companies never ask for payment during recruitment
  • Be cautious of interviews conducted only over phone/WhatsApp
  • Verify company address and contact information

💳 Credit Card & Loan Scams

Fake pre-approved credit cards or instant loans that require processing fees or personal document sharing.

Handle Credit Offers Carefully:

  • Apply for credit cards only through official bank channels
  • No legitimate bank charges fees for pre-approved offers
  • Check your credit score through authorized platforms
  • Don't share credit card details over phone calls

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Scams Targeting Parents

🎓 Fake School/College Admission Scams

Fraudulent admission offers for prestigious schools or colleges requiring donation payments or capitation fees through unofficial channels.

Ensure Legitimate Admissions:

  • Always go through official admission processes
  • Pay fees only through official school/college accounts
  • Be wary of guaranteed admission offers for money
  • Verify admission counselors and their credentials

🚨 Child Emergency Scams

Scammers claim your child is in trouble, arrested, or hurt and needs immediate money transfer. They may even have some personal information about your child.

Handle Child Emergency Calls:

  • Always try to contact your child directly first
  • Ask specific questions only your child would know
  • Contact the school or other parents to verify the situation
  • Stay calm and don't make hasty financial decisions

🛍️ Online Shopping Scams for Kids' Items

Fake online stores selling children's items, toys, or educational materials at very low prices but never delivering products.

Shop Safely Online:

  • Use well-known e-commerce platforms like Amazon, Flipkart
  • Check seller ratings and reviews before purchasing
  • Be cautious of deals that seem too good to be true
  • Use secure payment methods and avoid direct bank transfers
